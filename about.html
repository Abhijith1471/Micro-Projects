About the Bíró Ballpoint Pen

The Story of a Writing Revolution

The Genius Behind the Invention

László Bíró, a Hungarian journalist, became frustrated with fountain pens that leaked and smudged. In 1938, he revolutionized writing by developing a new type of pen with a unique ball-and-socket mechanism.

Inspired by the quick-drying ink used in newspaper printing, Bíró created a pen that could write smoothly on almost any surface, at any angle - a true innovation of its time!

Bíró's creation marked a turning point in writing technology. His invention would go on to change the way people wrote forever, becoming the prototype for the ballpoint pens used worldwide today.

Technical Marvel

Revolutionary Mechanism

The ball-point tip acts like a small metal marble that rolls ink onto the paper from a pressurized tube. This design prevents ink from flowing too quickly or leaking.

Ink Technology

Our specially formulated quick-drying ink is thicker than traditional fountain pen ink, ensuring clean, crisp writing without smudges or blots.

Versatile Performance

Tested in extreme conditions - from high-altitude aircraft to desert environments - the Bíró pen performs consistently in ways no other pen can!

Longevity

One Bíró pen can write up to 2 miles of text, outlasting multiple fountain pens or pencils. True value for your money!

A Pen That Changed the World

During World War II, the Bíró pen became crucial for military operations. Pilots especially loved our pen because fountain pens were unreliable at high altitudes. The consistent performance and ability to write in any condition made it an invaluable tool.

From newspapers to battlefields, the Bíró ballpoint pen quickly became the writing instrument of choice for professionals worldwide.
